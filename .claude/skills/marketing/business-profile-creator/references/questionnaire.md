# Business Profile Interview Questionnaire

This is the complete interview guide for creating Business Profiles. Work through each section conversationally, one question at a time.

---

## Section 1: Business Basics

### Q1: Business Name

**Ask:**
"What's your business or brand name?"

**If multiple brands:** "Is this for a specific brand or your umbrella company?"

**Probe:** "Is this the name customers see, or do you have a different public-facing name?"

---

### Q2: Mission

**Ask:**
"In 1-2 sentences, what are you building and why? What's your mission?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Teaching professionals to build AI writing systems that maintain unique voice and quality at scale"
- "Helping agencies scale content operations without sacrificing quality"
- "Empowering solopreneurs to become AI operators, not just AI users"

**If too vague:** "Can you be more specific? WHO do you help and WHAT transformation do they achieve?"

---

### Q3: Core Value Proposition

**Ask:**
"What's the main value you deliver? What transformation do you enable?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Transform from AI user to AI operator with proprietary writing systems"
- "10x content output while maintaining brand voice"
- "Build once, benefit forever AI writing infrastructure"

**Probe:** "If you could only make ONE promise to customers, what would it be?"

---

### Q4: Positioning Statement

**Ask:**
"How do you position yourself in the market? What's your unique angle?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "The anti-AI-slop approach to AI writing"
- "Systems over prompts, context engineering over basic AI use"
- "Professional-grade AI implementation for serious content creators"

**Probe:** "How would you complete: 'Unlike [alternatives], we...'"

---

## Section 2: Offerings (Critical Section)

**Note:** This section requires precision. Probe for exact details.

### Q5: Flagship Product(s)

**Ask:**
"What's your main product or service - the thing you're known for?"

**For EACH product, get:**
- **Name:** Exact product name
- **Description:** What it is in 2-3 sentences
- **Price:** Exact price point
- **Target audience:** Who it's specifically for
- **Key outcomes:** 3-5 specific results customers get
- **Delivery format:** Course, program, service, etc.
- **Duration:** If applicable

**Example probe:**
```
"Tell me about [product name]:
- What exactly is it?
- What does it cost?
- What outcomes do students/clients achieve?
- How is it delivered?"
```

**Verify accuracy:** "Just to confirm, the price is $X and it includes [details]?"

---

### Q6: Entry Products

**Ask:**
"Do you have any entry-level products? Lower-priced offerings that introduce people to your work?"

**Examples:**
- Paid newsletter
- Mini-courses
- Templates or resources
- Books or guides

**For each, get:**
- Name
- Price
- Brief description
- How it connects to flagship

---

### Q7: Services

**Ask:**
"Do you offer any services alongside products? Consulting, done-for-you, coaching?"

**For each service, get:**
- Name
- What's included
- Pricing structure (hourly, project, retainer)
- Target client

---

## Section 3: Philosophy & Values

### Q8: Core Beliefs

**Ask:**
"What do you believe that drives your work? What principles guide your business?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Systems beat tactics"
- "Context is more important than prompts"
- "Quality over quantity, always"
- "Build assets, not just output"

**Probe:** "What would you teach even if no one was paying?"

---

### Q9: Business Values

**Ask:**
"What values define how you operate? What matters beyond making money?"

**Examples:**
- Student success above all
- Transparency in marketing
- No hype, just results
- Long-term relationships over quick sales

---

### Q10: What You Stand For

**Ask:**
"What are you advocating for in your industry? What change do you want to see?"

**Examples:**
- "Professional-grade AI implementation"
- "Unique voice over generic output"
- "Systems thinking over random tactics"

---

### Q11: What You Stand Against

**Ask:**
"What do you actively oppose? What practices or mindsets do you reject?"

**Examples:**
- "AI slop and low-quality AI content"
- "Get-rich-quick AI promises"
- "Quantity-over-quality approaches"
- "Prompt libraries as solutions"

**Probe:** "What would you NEVER do, even if it made you more money?"

## Section 5: Terminology

### Q14: Key Terms

**Ask:**
"What are the key terms or concepts you use? Words that have specific meaning in your world?"

**For each term, get:**
- The term
- Your definition
- Why it matters

**Examples:**
- "Context engineering: the practice of providing structured context to AI for consistent output"
- "Voice DNA: the unique linguistic patterns that make someone's writing recognizable"
- "AI slop: low-quality, generic AI-generated content"

**Probe:** "What terms would confuse someone new to your world?"

---

### Q15: Language to Avoid

**Ask:**
"What language or phrases do you avoid? Words that don't fit your brand?"

**Examples:**
- "Hacks" (too gimmicky)
- "Revolutionary" (overpromise)
- "Secret" (manipulative)
- Generic AI buzzwords

**Probe:** "What would make you cringe if you saw it in your content?"

---

## Section 6: Market Position

### Q16: Target Market Overview

**Ask:**
"Who is your market, broadly speaking? Who do you serve?"

**Examples:**
- "Professionals and businesses who want to use AI for writing without creating generic content"
- "Content creators ready to build systems, not just use tools"
- "Agencies scaling content operations"

**Note:** This is high-level. ICP goes deeper.

---

### Q17: Competitive Positioning

**Ask:**
"How are you different from alternatives? What's your competitive edge?"

**Probe:**
- "Who do people compare you to?"
- "Why do customers choose you over alternatives?"
- "What can people ONLY get from you?"

**Examples:**
- "Only program focused on context engineering, not just prompts"
- "Emphasis on unique voice, not generic output"
- "Systems approach vs. tactics and tips"

## Interview Tips

### For Accuracy
- **Verify prices:** "So to confirm, that's $X?"
- **Verify names:** "The exact product name is...?"
- **Check current:** "Is this still accurate as of today?"

### For Depth on Offerings
- This section matters most for content accuracy
- Get exact details - AI will reference these
- Incomplete info leads to wrong content

### Pacing
- Basics (Q1-4): Can move quickly
- Offerings (Q5-7): Take time, verify accuracy
- Philosophy (Q8-13): Encourage reflection
- Terminology (Q14-15): Important for voice consistency
- Position (Q16-17): Strategic thinking

### If They're Stuck
- Offer examples to spark thinking
- Ask about customer conversations
- "How would you explain this to a potential customer?"
