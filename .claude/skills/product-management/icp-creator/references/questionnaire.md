# ICP Interview Questionnaire

This is the complete interview guide for creating Ideal Customer Profiles. Work through each section conversationally, one question at a time.

---

## Section 1: Profile Basics

### Q1: Profile Name

**Ask:**
"Let's start by naming this customer segment. What should we call them?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Professional AI Operators"
- "Agency Owners Scaling Content"
- "Solopreneur Content Creators"
- "B2B Marketing Leaders"

**If too broad:** "That's pretty broad. Can we narrow it down? Instead of 'entrepreneurs,' maybe 'content entrepreneurs' or 'service-based entrepreneurs'?"

**If stuck:** "Think about how you'd describe your best customers to a friend. What makes them distinct?"

---

### Q2: One-Line Description

**Ask:**
"In one sentence, what defines this person? Complete this: 'Someone who...'"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Someone who understands AI's potential but is frustrated by generic output"
- "Someone who wants to scale content without sacrificing quality"
- "Someone who's tried AI tools but can't maintain their unique voice"

**If too vague:** "What's the ONE thing that makes them different from everyone else in their field?"

---

## Section 2: Demographics

### Q3: Professional Roles

**Ask:**
"What job titles or roles does this person typically have? List 3-5."

**Examples to offer:**
- Content strategists, marketing directors
- Agency owners, consultants
- Solopreneurs, course creators
- CMOs, brand managers

**Probe for specificity:** "Are these in-house or agency? B2B or B2C? What industries?"

---

### Q4: Experience Level

**Ask:**
"Where are they in their career or AI journey? Beginner, intermediate, or advanced?"

**Clarify if needed:**
- Beginner: Just starting, overwhelmed by options
- Intermediate: Tried things, have some experience, but stuck
- Advanced: Know the basics, want next-level implementation

**Follow-up:** "What have they already tried or accomplished?"

---

### Q5: Business Stage

**Ask:**
"What stage is their business or career at? Select all that apply."

**Options to offer:**
- Just starting out (0-1 years)
- Growing/scaling (1-3 years)
- Established, looking to optimize (3+ years)
- Enterprise/corporate role
- Transitioning careers

**Follow-up:** "What resources do they have - time, money, team?"

---

## Section 3: Psychographics (The Deep Stuff)

### Q6: Core Values

**Ask:**
"What do they care about deeply? What principles guide their decisions?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Quality over quantity
- Authenticity and originality
- Efficiency without shortcuts
- Building lasting assets
- Professional reputation

**Probe:** "What would they NEVER compromise on, even if it cost them money or time?"

---

### Q7: Frustrations

**Ask:**
"What frustrates them daily? What makes them think 'there HAS to be a better way'?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "AI output sounds robotic and generic"
- "Spending hours editing AI content"
- "Can't maintain consistent voice"
- "Competitors all sound the same"

**Probe for depth:** "What's the emotional weight of this frustration? How does it make them feel?"

**Get specific:** "Can you share an actual quote or complaint you've heard?"

---

### Q8: Fears

**Ask:**
"What keeps them up at night? What are they afraid will happen if they don't solve this?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Falling behind competitors
- Damaging reputation with low-quality content
- Wasting time on approaches that don't work
- Missing the AI opportunity window
- Losing their unique voice

**Probe:** "What's the worst-case scenario they imagine?"

---

### Q9: Aspirations

**Ask:**
"What do they dream of achieving? Paint the picture of success for them."

**Examples to offer:**
- "Create AI content indistinguishable from my best work"
- "Build systems that scale without quality loss"
- "Become known for exceptional AI implementation"
- "10x content output while maintaining uniqueness"

**Probe:** "If they solved this perfectly, what would their life/business look like in 6-12 months?"

---

## Section 4: Current vs. Desired State

### Q10: What They've Tried

**Ask:**
"What have they already tried to solve this problem?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Basic ChatGPT/Claude prompting
- Prompt libraries and templates
- AI writing tools (Jasper, Copy.ai, etc.)
- YouTube tutorials
- Generic AI courses

**Follow-up:** "How much time/money have they invested in these attempts?"

---

### Q11: Why It Hasn't Worked

**Ask:**
"Why haven't those solutions worked for them?"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Prompts give inconsistent results"
- "Output lacks unique voice"
- "No systematic approach"
- "Tools don't understand specific context"
- "More time editing than writing"

**Probe:** "What's the gap between what they expected and what they got?"

---

### Q12: Transformation Vision

**Ask:**
"Describe their ideal end state. What does 'solved' look like?"

**Frame it as:** "Complete this: 'After working with you, they will be able to...'"

**Examples to offer:**
- "Produce 10x content without quality drop"
- "Have AI that actually sounds like them"
- "Deploy systems that run without constant babysitting"
- "Differentiate completely from AI-slop competitors"

**Probe:** "How will they KNOW they've achieved this? What's the proof?"

---

## Section 5: Language & Communication

### Q13: How They Describe Problems

**Ask:**
"What exact words do they use to describe their problem? Quote them if possible."

**Examples to offer:**
- "My AI content sounds robotic"
- "I'm creating AI slop like everyone else"
- "I can't maintain my voice at scale"
- "I need professional-grade AI writing"

**Probe:** "What would they type into Google to solve this?"

**Probe:** "How would they explain this to a colleague?"

---

### Q14: Trigger Words

**Ask:**
"What words or phrases make them pay attention? What do they respond to?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Systems, frameworks, implementation
- Professional, unique, differentiated
- Scale, automate, streamline
- Context, voice, brand
- Proven, specific, actionable

**Probe:** "What words would make them click on an email subject line?"

---

## Section 6: Objections & Barriers

### Q15: Main Objections

**Ask:**
"What objections do they raise before buying or committing? List 5-8."

**Categories to explore:**

**Skepticism:**
- "I've tried AI writing and it's always mediocre"
- "Won't everyone have access to these same systems?"
- "How is this different from prompt engineering?"

**Practical concerns:**
- "Do I have time to build these systems?"
- "Is this too technical for me?"
- "Will this work for my industry?"

**Investment hesitation:**
- "Is this worth the investment?"
- "Can I figure this out on my own?"
- "What if I don't get results?"

**Probe:** "Which objection is the hardest to overcome?"

---

### Q16: What Convinces Them

**Ask:**
"What finally convinces them to take action? What proof do they need?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Before/after content examples
- Specific frameworks they'll receive
- Case studies and testimonials
- Demonstrations of systems in action
- Clear ROI calculations

**Probe:** "What's the moment they decide 'I'm in'? What tips them over?"

---

## Section 7: Behavior & Decision-Making

### Q17: Information Consumption

**Ask:**
"Where do they learn and consume information? How do they stay current?"

**Examples to offer:**
- Twitter/LinkedIn thought leaders
- Newsletters and email courses
- YouTube tutorials
- Podcasts
- Communities and Slack groups
- Courses and training programs

**Probe:** "Which sources do they TRUST most?"

---

### Q18: Decision Timeline

**Ask:**
"How long do they take to make purchasing decisions? Days, weeks, months?"

**Probe:**
- "Do they decide quickly once convinced, or need time to research?"
- "Do they need to consult others (boss, partner, team)?"
- "What accelerates their decision?"

---

### Q19: Investment Mindset

**Ask:**
"How do they think about investing in solutions like yours?"

**Explore:**
- Price sensitivity (low, medium, high)
- Value vs. cost orientation
- Payment preferences (one-time, subscription)
- ROI expectations

**Probe:** "What's the conversation in their head when they see your price?"

---

## Interview Tips

### Pacing
- Don't rush - let them think
- One question at a time
- Acknowledge good answers ("That's really specific, thank you")

### Depth Over Speed
- Surface answers aren't useful
- Push for examples and quotes
- "Can you be more specific?" is your friend

### If They're Stuck
- Offer examples to spark thinking
- Ask about real conversations they've had
- "Think about your last 3 customers..."

### Validation
- Repeat back what you heard
- "So what I'm hearing is..."
- Confirm before moving on
